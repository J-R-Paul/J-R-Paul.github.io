On optimizing for serendipity

February 4, 2024

Most interesting things in my life have come from serendipitous encounters. I've started to intentionally design my life to increase the probability of these moments— living in dense areas, attending events outside my comfort zone, saying yes to random invitations. The ROI has been consistently high.

Better interfaces > more information

January 15, 2024

We already have access to more information than we can possibly consume, yet we often struggle to access the right information at the right time. The bottleneck isn't more data, but better interfaces to the data we already have. This is why I'm so interested in tools for thought.

City noise vs. natural noise

December 5, 2023

Our brains process city noise (traffic, construction) differently than natural noise (waves, birds). City noise demands our attention, triggers stress responses, and is unpredictable in unhelpful ways. Natural noise tends to follow patterns our brains evolved alongside, creating a soundscape that can be both stimulating and relaxing.

Reading as conversation

November 20, 2023

The best reading experiences feel like conversations with the author. When I write in the margins, underline passages, and reflect on what I've read, I'm engaging in a dialogue across time and space. This is why I'm skeptical of speed reading techniques that focus solely on information extraction. They miss the conversational aspect that makes reading rewarding.

Thinking in public

October 8, 2023

There's something powerful about thinking in public—writing blog posts, tweeting ideas, having conversations in open forums. It forces clarity, invites feedback, and creates unexpected connections. The internet has dramatically lowered the cost of thinking in public, but we're still figuring out the norms and practices that make it most valuable.
